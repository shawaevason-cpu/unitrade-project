Stay Safe on UniTrade

UniTrade connects LUANAR students — but your safety always comes first. Read these guidelines before buying or selling anything on campus.

Safety Guidelines

🤝
Meeting in Person
Rules for safe face-to-face exchanges on campus
1
Always meet on campus in a well-populated public area — Library entrance, Student Centre, Cafeteria, or Administration Block.
2
Bring a friend. Having someone with you significantly reduces risk and gives you a witness.
3
Meet during busy hours — daytime, not late evening. Avoid isolated spots, residences, or off-campus locations.
4
Tell someone where you're going. Share the seller's name, listing link, and expected meeting time with a trusted person.
5
Trust your instincts. If anything feels wrong — cancel and leave. No item is worth your safety.
💳
Payment & Money
How to pay safely and avoid scams
1
Never pay in advance. Always pay only after you have physically received and inspected the item.
2
Avoid mobile money transfers until you're holding the item. Transfers are hard to reverse if the seller disappears.
3
Do not share your banking PIN, Mpamba PIN, Airtel Money PIN, or any OTP codes with anyone — ever.
4
Beware of "send a deposit" requests. Legitimate sellers don't ask for partial payment to hold an item for strangers.
5
Use exact cash where possible. Avoid creating change disputes. Count money in the open before exchanging.
🔍
Verifying Items
Inspect before you commit to buying
1
Inspect thoroughly before paying. Test electronics, check textbook editions, try on clothing, verify serial numbers.
2
Compare with the listing photos. If the item looks different from what was advertised, walk away.
3
Ask for proof of purchase for high-value electronics — a receipt or original box confirms legitimacy.
4
Don't be pressured. Sellers who rush you or won't let you inspect properly are a red flag.
5
Textbooks: Verify the edition, ISBN, and that all pages are present before agreeing on price.
💬
Online Communication
Staying safe before you meet
1
Keep negotiations on UniTrade (comments) or WhatsApp — never move to unknown platforms or third-party apps.
2
Verify identity via WhatsApp call before meeting. A voice call is harder to fake than text messages.
3
Check their profile. A verified LUANAR Student ID, profile photo, and listing history all increase credibility.
4
Screenshot all conversations before meeting. If anything goes wrong, you'll have a record.
5
Be wary of new accounts with no history posting suspiciously cheap high-value items.
⚠️
Recognising Scams
Common tricks and how to avoid them
1
Price too good to be true? A MK 200,000 laptop listed for MK 30,000 is almost certainly a scam or stolen goods.
2
"I'm not on campus right now" — sellers who can't meet in person and ask you to pay first are almost always scammers.
3
Fake screenshots of payment can be easily created. Always confirm a transfer on your own device or check your balance.
4
Urgency pressure — "I'm leaving campus tomorrow, buy today" is a classic pressure tactic. Legitimate sellers are patient.
5
Stolen goods — if a seller can't explain where an item came from, or the price is unrealistically low, do not buy.
🏷️
Selling Safely
Protect yourself as a seller
1
Only accept verified payment. Wait until the transfer reflects in your own account before handing over the item.
2
Don't give your home address. Arrange all hand-overs in public campus locations, not your room or residence.
3
Photograph the item before the exchange to document its condition in case of any dispute.
4
Be honest about condition. Clearly state defects in your listing. Misleading buyers violates UniTrade's Terms.
5
Block and report buyers who are aggressive, threatening, or make unreasonable demands after a fair listing.
